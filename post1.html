From Contamination to Control

After two weeks of careful cell culture work, I encountered something every researcher dreads — contamination.

The entire culture had to be discarded.

At first, I questioned every step. Media preparation, aseptic handling, incubation everything seemed correct.

Yet, failure happened.

This experience reinforced a critical lesson:

Science demands not just accuracy, but consistency and discipline.

It pushed me to refine my techniques, strengthen biosafety practices, and pay attention to even the smallest details.

Every contamination is not just a loss — it is a lesson.

Every restart builds a better researcher.
