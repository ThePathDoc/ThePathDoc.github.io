From Contamination to Control

Sometimes, science teaches us the most through setbacks.

After nearly two weeks of carefully growing a cell culture, I faced an unfortunate reality — contamination had taken over the entire stock, and it had to be discarded.

It was frustrating. But more importantly, it was a moment to pause and reflect.

I went back to every step:

✔ Reviewed my protocol
✔ Cross-checked my documentation
✔ Reflected on each action performed

Surprisingly, everything seemed to be done right. Yet, contamination still occurred — a reminder that in research, not everything is always within our control.

This experience made me deeply appreciate the discipline I developed while working in a GLP & AAALAC-accredited lab. Maintaining detailed, structured records wasn’t just a requirement — it became a habit that now provides clarity and confidence during moments like this.

Key GLP Principles I Reflected On:

🔬 Readiness of Data – Always being prepared with clear, complete records
🔍 Retraceability – The ability to trace every step back to its source
🔁 Reproducibility – Ensuring research can be reliably repeated

Now, I’ve restarted the culture with even stricter biosafety measures and renewed attention to detail.

Setbacks are not failures — they are checkpoints that refine our approach and strengthen our mindset.

Looking forward to applying these GLP principles even more rigorously in my research journey.
